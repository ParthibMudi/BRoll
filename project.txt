Welcome, Future Builders
We are exploring a product idea that helps people create professional short videos without needing
advanced editing skills.
A raw talking-head video often feels plain. Even if the speaker has a good point, the video may not
feel polished because there are no supporting visuals.
Good short videos often use:
- Text highlights
- Infographic slides
- Simple explainers
- Lists
- Decision trees
- Before/after visuals
- B-roll inserts
Your assignment is to build a proof-of-concept that takes a transcript or script, strategically identifies
where visual explanation is useful, generates infographic-style PPT slides for those selected
moments, converts those slides into video clips, and merges them with the original video.
The goal is not to create random slides.
The goal is to prove that the right visual, shown at the right moment, can make a spoken idea easier
to understand.


Project Context
The larger product vision is:
User records or uploads a video
↓
System creates a timestamped transcript
↓
System identifies where visual support is actually needed
↓
AI suggests infographic B-roll concepts
↓
PPT templates are filled programmatically
↓
Slides are converted into image/video clips
↓
B-roll clips are inserted into the original video
↓
A more professional final video is generated
Your group is responsible for the visual explanation layer.
You are not expected to build the AI interviewer or the Descript-style word editor.
Your focus is only:


Timestamped transcript/script
↓
Strategic B-roll selection
↓
B-roll concept
↓
PPT slide
↓
B-roll video
↓
Merged final video

Please use this stack unless you have a strong reason to use another one:
Backend / Script:
Node.js or Python

pptxgenjs if using Node.js
or python-pptx if using Python

Local file system

The main requirement is not the stack.
The main requirement is:
Can your system convert selected spoken ideas into useful infographic B-roll and merge them into
the video?


Objective
Build a working POC where the system can:


1. Take a timestamped transcript or script
2. Decide which sentences or groups of sentences actually need B-roll
3. Skip ordinary sentences that do not need visual explanation
4. Identify 1–3 strong B-roll-worthy moments
5. Choose a suitable infographic template
6. Generate PPT slides programmatically
7. Convert those slides into image/video clips
8. Insert or overlay the B-roll into the original video
9. Produce a final combined video
The final demo should prove one key thing:
The system can turn important spoken ideas into visual explainers automatically.

Important B-Roll Selection Principle
Not every sentence needs an explainer.
The goal is not to create one slide for every line of transcript.
The goal is to identify strategic moments where a visual can make the spoken idea easier to
understand, remember, or feel more polished.
A good B-roll moment usually appears when the speaker is saying something like:
- A key insight
- A list of reasons
- A comparison
- A process
- A decision point
- A before/after idea
- A problem/solution explanation
- A concept that may be easier to understand visually
A sentence may not need B-roll if it is:
- Just a greeting
- A personal filler line
- A transition sentence
- Repetition of an earlier point
- Already clear without visual support
- Too small or weak to deserve a slide
The B-roll agent should be selective.
For a 30–60 second demo clip, it is enough to identify:
1–3 strong B-roll moments


A good final video should still feel like a talking-head video supported by visuals, not like a slideshow
replacing the speaker.


Input Requirement
Use any spoken video longer than 5 minutes.
For the demo, you may use a selected 30–60 second portion.
The video should contain at least one idea that can be explained visually.
Examples:
- Why people avoid making videos
- Why small businesses struggle with software
- Why hotel staff prefer voice over forms
- Why CRMs feel heavy for solo sellers
- Why students struggle to complete coding projects

Part 2: Strategic B-Roll Concept Generator
What to Build
Create a system or agent that reads the transcript and identifies where visual support would improve
the video.
The system should first decide:
Does this sentence or group of sentences actually need visual explanation?
Only if the answer is yes should it create a B-roll item.
The system should decide:
- Which section deserves visual explanation
- Which sections should be skipped
- What idea should be shown visually
- Which template fits the idea
- What text should appear on the slide
- When the B-roll should appear
- Why this moment was selected
For the POC, support only 2 templates:
1. Four-point list
2. Decision tree
You may add a third template if time permits:
3. Problem → Solution

Part 3: PPT Template Generator
What to Build
Create PPT templates that can be filled programmatically.
Minimum templates:
1. Four-point list template
2. Decision-tree template
The PPT generation should take JSON as input and replace text dynamically.
Example input:
{
"template_type": "four_point_list",
"title": "Why Voice Works Better",
"points": [
"Natural for staff",
"Less training needed",
"Faster updates",
"Better adoption"
]
}
Expected output:
Generated PPT slide with:
- Title
- Four points
- Clean visual layout


For the decision-tree template, expected input may look like:
{
"template_type": "decision_tree",
"title": "Does This System Reduce Work?",
"nodes": [
{
"label": "Adds more steps?",
"yes": "Feels like burden",
"no": "Feels useful"
}
]
}
Expected output:
Generated PPT slide with:
- Title
- One decision question
- Yes branch
- No branch
The slide does not need to be extremely beautiful, but it should look clear and professional enough
for a demo.


Part 4: Convert PPT to B-Roll Video
What to Build
Convert the generated PPT slide into an image or video clip.
Acceptable approaches:
- Export slide as image
- Export slide as short video
- Convert PPT to PDF/image and then to video
- Use ffmpeg to create a 5–8 second video clip from the slide image
For example:
PPT slide → PNG image → 6-second MP4 clip
This is good enough for the POC.


Part 5: Merge B-Roll with Original Video
What to Build
Use ffmpeg or another video processing tool to combine the B-roll with the original video.
You may choose either approach:


Option 1:
Insert B-roll full-screen between talking-head sections
Option 2:
Overlay B-roll on top of the original video for a few seconds
Option 3:
Show talking-head video on one side and B-roll on the other side
For a 2-day POC, the simplest approach is acceptable:
Original talking video
↓
B-roll image/video appears full-screen for selected timestamp
↓
Original talking video continues
Small jump cuts are acceptable.
The important thing is that B-roll appears at the right moment and supports the spoken idea.
